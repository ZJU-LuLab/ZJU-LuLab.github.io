Dr. Lu Lu is currently an Assistant Professor in the Department of Engineering Mechanics at Zhejiang University. He received his B.S. and Ph.D. degrees from Ningbo University and Shanghai University in China in 2014 and 2019, respectively. He then worked as a postdoctoral researcher at Peking University and Stanford University from 2020 to 2026 before joining Zhejiang University. His research interests focus on solid mechanics, with emphasis on mechanical instabilities, deployable structures, and mechanics of intelligent soft materials. He has published nearly 30 peer-reviewed papers in journals such as PNAS, JMPS, IJSS, AMR, IJMS, JAM, and PRSA, and received the ASME Melville Medal in 2024.

## Research Interests

- Mechanical instabilities, including buckling, snap-through, bifurcation, and post-buckling behavior
- Soft active materials, including liquid crystal elastomers, magnetic soft materials, and stimuli-responsive polymers
- Elastic rods, rings, plates, and shells
- Deployable, reconfigurable, and morphing structures
- Mechanics-guided design for soft robotics and functional materials
